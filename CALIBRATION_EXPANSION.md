## Calibration Multipliers

### Economic Sentiment Multipliers
| Context | Multiplier | Application |
|---------|------------|-------------|
| Consumer optimism stated | 0.85x | Discount stated optimism by 15% |
| Purchase intent vs actual | 0.60-0.75x | Intent overstates actual purchases |
| Savings claims vs reality | 0.90x | Slight optimism bias |
| "Plan to buy" statements | 0.65x | Significant intent-action gap |

### Demographic Multipliers
| Factor | Adjustment |
|--------|------------|
| Gen Z stated intent | 1.1x (higher follow-through on digital) |
| Boomer stated intent | 0.95x (more conservative actual behavior) |
| High-income ($100K+) | 1.15x (higher conversion) |
| Low-income (<$25K) | 0.70x (financial constraints) |
| Urban vs Rural | 1.05x / 0.90x respectively |

### Category-Specific Multipliers
| Category | Stated→Actual Multiplier |
|----------|-------------------------|
| Travel intent | 0.80x |
| Home buying intent | 0.55x (financial barriers) |
| EV purchase intent | 0.70x |
| Sustainability premium willingness | 0.50x |
| Streaming subscription intent | 0.85x |
| Restaurant visit frequency | 0.75x |

### Trust-Based Multipliers
| Trust Level | Satisfaction Adjustment |
|-------------|------------------------|
| High trust | +1.4x satisfaction scores |
| Low trust | Base (1.0x) |
| Trust decline trend | -0.05x per year |

## Coverage Gaps & Future Research

### Well-Covered Domains ✓
- Consumer economic sentiment
- Financial well-being & savings
- Social media usage
- Healthcare costs & access
- Employee engagement
- Generational attitudes
- Media consumption patterns
- Auto/banking/hotel satisfaction

### Partial Coverage ⚠️
- **Automotive purchasing**: Have EV intent data, need more ICE preferences
- **Sustainability**: US data available, need deeper demographic cuts
- **Food/dining**: Have frequency data, need cuisine/price preferences
- **Travel**: Airlines/hotels covered, need experiences/activities

### Significant Gaps ❌
1. **B2B Decision-Making**: No calibration data for business purchasing
2. **Healthcare Provider Choice**: Limited data on how patients select providers
3. **Insurance Shopping Behavior**: Need detailed decision factors
4. **Real Estate Regional Variations**: National data only

## Methodology Notes

### Source Quality Tiers

**Tier 1 - Gold Standard** (Highest reliability)
- Federal Reserve SHED: Government survey, large sample, rigorous methodology
- Pew Research NPORS: Multi-mode, probability sampling, transparent methods
- Kaiser Family Foundation: Long-running, methodologically sound
- Gallup: Established methodology, consistent tracking

**Tier 2 - High Quality**
- McKinsey ConsumerWise: Large samples, consistent methodology
- JD Power Studies: Industry standard, large samples
- Deloitte Surveys: Professional methodology, large samples
- Conference Board: Established economic indicators

**Tier 3 - Good Quality**
- Morning Consult: Fast-turnaround but reliable
- YouGov: Online panels, good demographic coverage
- Ipsos: Global reach, varied methodologies
- Harris Poll: Long-running, respected

### Using This Data for Calibration

1. **Cross-reference multiple sources** when available
2. **Apply demographic multipliers** based on target population
3. **Account for intent-action gaps** using category multipliers
4. **Consider recency** - prefer data within 12-18 months
5. **Note methodology changes** (e.g., Pew's mode shift in 2023)
